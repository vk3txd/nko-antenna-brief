# Trademarks and Brand Usage

This document explains how you may (and may not) use the **project names, callsign, and branding** associated with the work published by **VK3TXD** on GitHub.

> **Not legal advice.** This is a practical community policy intended to prevent confusion, misattribution, and brand dilution while keeping the technical work freely usable.

## What this covers

The following are considered **brand identifiers** (“Marks”) for this ecosystem (whether registered or not):

- **VK3TXD** (callsign / handle) and any VK3TXD logos or badges
- **NKO** and **New Kallista OCF**
- Any other project names used in this organisation/repository (as listed in the relevant README)
- “Kallista” (when used as part of project branding, e.g., “New Kallista …”)

## Summary

- You **may** use the Marks to **refer** to this project (documentation, reviews, comparisons, academic/club articles, videos).
- You **may** build, sell, and commercially distribute products based on the project **content** (subject to the repository’s LICENSE and attribution requirements).
- You **may not** use the Marks in a way that implies **endorsement**, **official status**, or that your product *is* the official NKO (or other) project.
- You **may not** name your derived antenna/product **NKO** (or confusingly similar) without written permission.

## Allowed uses (examples)

### 1) Referencing the project (nominative use)
- “Based on the **NKO – New Kallista OCF** design by **VK3TXD**.”
- “We tested an antenna derived from **NKO**.”
- “This kit is **compatible with** the NKO build guide.”

### 2) Commercial use with clear attribution (and no confusion)
- “Commercial kit derived from the NKO design (VK3TXD). **Not affiliated with or endorsed by VK3TXD**.”
- “Antenna insulator set for NKO-style builds (credit: VK3TXD).”

### 3) Comparisons and commentary
- “NKO vs OCF vs EFHW A/B test results.”
- “Common-mode current notes, referencing VK3TXD’s work.”

## Not allowed (examples)

- Naming your product: **“NKO Antenna”** (or “New Kallista OCF”) as if it is the official product.
- Using a name/logo that is **confusingly similar** (e.g., “NKO-Pro”, “Kallista OCF Official”, “VK3TXD Certified”) without permission.
- Using the Marks on packaging, listings, or advertising in a way that suggests:
  - endorsement by VK3TXD,
  - official status,
  - a partnership,
  - or that your product is the original project.

## Naming guidance for derived or compatible products

If you sell or publish a derivative, use wording like:

- “Derived from **NKO – New Kallista OCF** by **VK3TXD**.”
- “NKO-compatible (design credit: VK3TXD).”
- “Off-centre-fed dipole inspired by VK3TXD’s NKO concept.”

Avoid using “NKO” as the **primary** product name. Use it only as a reference, and include a **clear non-endorsement** line.

## Non-endorsement requirement

Unless you have explicit written permission, you must not imply that VK3TXD endorses, certifies, or supports your product or publication.

Recommended disclaimer line:

> “This work/product is independent and **not affiliated with or endorsed by VK3TXD**.”

## Logos and badges

Do not use VK3TXD logos/badges (or any “official-looking” emblems) without permission.

## Permission requests

If you want to:
- use “NKO” (or other Marks) in a product name,
- use logos/badges,
- claim “official” status,
- or create a branded commercial offering,

please request permission by opening an issue or discussion in the relevant GitHub repository describing:
- what you want to do,
- where it will appear (listing/site/packaging),
- and how attribution and non-endorsement will be shown.

## How this interacts with the LICENSE

The project’s **LICENSE** grants rights to use the *content* (docs, designs, code) under its terms.  
This **TRADEMARKS.md** governs only the *brand identifiers* (names, marks, logos) to reduce confusion and protect provenance.
